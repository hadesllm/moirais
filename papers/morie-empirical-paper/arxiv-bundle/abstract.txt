This paper is the empirical application companion to the MRM (McNamara—Ruhela—Medina) framework (Ruhela2026MRM) and its open-source software realisation in the morie toolkit (Python and R; Ruhela2026MorieR, Ruhela2026MoriePy). Two anchor datasets are analysed under the MRM modules: the Ontario Tracking Information System a01 restrictive- confinement detailed dataset ( a01, fiscal years 2023—2025; n = 76,934 person-day records over 65,467 unique person-year identifiers; the b01 segregation-placement file, n = 82,001 placements, is used for the per-placement analyses of §phys-goffman), and the Toronto Police Service open-data major-crime corpus (). On b01, a double-machine-learning interactive regression model (IRM-DML) estimates the effect of alert complexity (treatment 2) on placement volatility (count of regional transfers per person-year ), pooled across 2023—2025 and by fiscal year. The pooled estimate is _ATE = 0.1605 (SE = 0.00628, 95\ the per-year breakdown shows a monotone rising trend (_ ATE,2023 = 0.1342; _ATE,2024 = 0.1591; _ATE,2025 = 0.1737). Multi-way regional clustering shifts the point estimate into a tighter band [0.1932, 0.2013] across 30 region scheme combinations, with all p < 10^-24. Matched-sample Poisson GLMM (MatchIt 1:1 nearest-neighbour propensity-score matching followed by a random-intercept Poisson generalised linear mixed model) reproduces the direction of the effect as a sensitivity check. The empirical identifiability of cross-year recidivism on is confirmed impossible by data-dictionary inspection (0/65,467 identifiers appear in two or more fiscal years). On crime events, a non-Markovian Hawkes process specification (Gamma excitation kernel, sinusoidal baseline) is preferred over the classical Markovian Hawkes by Bayesian-information-criterion ranking and a higher Kolmogorov—Smirnov p$-value on the rescaled inter-event times. Every numerical claim in this paper is reproducible from the morie toolkit through the code blocks in Appendix app:reproducibility.
