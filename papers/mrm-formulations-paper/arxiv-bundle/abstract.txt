The MRM (Multilevel Reconciliation Methodology; people-credit reading: McNamara—Ruhela—Medina) framework provides a unified statistical foundation for analysing Canadian carceral, police and oversight data, integrating five public sources (provincial restrictive-confinement microdata, federal implementation reports, academic replications, Ontario police-oversight reports, and the open-data crime corpus). The framework couples a ten-estimator per-individual causal ensemble (IPW, AIPW, IRM-DML, PSM, PLR and AIPW-SuperLearner variants) with a ^2 family for aggregate tables, a Mandela Rules classifier operating at both federal and provincial level, and a Hawkes self-exciting process for spatio-temporal modelling. Implementation lives in the open-source MORIE toolkit (Ruhela2026MorieR; Ruhela2026MoriePy). Headline finding: a duration-only Mandela-prolonged proxy rises from $12.5\ provincial Ontario data; we report this alongside a definition- matched federal duration-only comparator and an explicit duration-plus-meaningful-contact provincial estimate to avoid the standard apples-to-oranges critique.
